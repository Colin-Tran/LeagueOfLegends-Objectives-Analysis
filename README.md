# League of Legends Analysis: A look into Objective Control and Won Games

League of Legends (LOL) is a wildly popular multiplayer online battle arena (MOBA) video game that was released in October 2009 by Riot Games. Since its release, it has grown into one of the largest video games in the world with millions of total players. With its growth has come multiple professional leagues worldwide, blooming the League of Legends Esports scene into one of the largest esports in the world.

Within League of Legends itself there is so much capturable in game data. Every match produces an enormous amount of data including kills, gold, vision control, early-game advantages, objectives, and so much more. We will be focusing on the specific dataset capturing all of the in game information from the LOL 2025 competitive season.

This dataset compiles data from over 40 different competitive leagues worldwide, comprising of over 118932 rows and 164 distinct columns. It captures a wide range of in-game statistics and match outcomes from professional League of Legends matches, including objectives, combat performance, vision metrics, economy statistics, and overall results.
